Visualizing Wikipedia: Working With Large Graphs

Max Adam Rachel

The Goal

For Palantir Hack Week, we wanted to build a pipeline which would allow someone to easily visualize portions of Wikipedia as a graph — we thought this would be cool for four reasons:

  • As someone researched a topic on Wikipedia they could use the graph visualization to help them choose what articles to read — a visual article recommender which would make research more efficient.
  • So that we could write a browser extension that would overlay your browser history on top of these graphs so that you could see topically what portions of Wikipedia you had read and compare your Wikipedia history with others.
  • Because Wikipedia might be the best proxy to a representation of all of human knowledge and visualizing it is interesting.
  • To learn more about current technologies for working with and analyzing graphs.

The Result

Most current visual graph technologies do not support We found that it was very difficult to make any graphs which would give real insight into the structure of Wikipedia — we had initially envisioned graphs of thousands of nodes visually displaying the relations between categories and their linked articles but as we dug deeper into the problem it became clear that most visual graph technologies are not built to handle graphs with > 1000 nodes.
